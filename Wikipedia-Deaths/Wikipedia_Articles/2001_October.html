The following is a list of notable deaths in October 2001.

Entries for each day are listed alphabetically by surname. A typical entry lists information in the following sequence:

  • Name, age, country of citizenship at birth, subsequent country of citizenship (if applicable), reason for notability, cause of death (if known), and reference.

October 2001[edit]

1[edit]

  • Guy Beaulne, 79, French-Canadian actor and theatre director.[1]
  • Anna McClean Bidder, 98, British zoologist and academic.
  • Surendranath Dwivedy, 88, Indian politician, journalist and social worker.
  • Kenny Greene, 32, American singer-songwriter, AIDS.
  • Gregory Hemingway, 69, American physician and son of Ernest Hemingway, hypertension and cardiovascular disease.
  • Mickey Trotman, 26, Trinidad and Tobago football player, car crash.

2[edit]

  • Manny Albam, 79, American jazz baritone saxophone player, composer, arranger and producer.
  • Pat Ast, 59, American actress and model.
  • Franz Biebl, 95, German classical music composer.[2]
  • Tommy McCulloch, 79, Scottish footballer.[3]
  • Seymour Milstein, 81, American real estate developer and philanthropist.

3[edit]

  • Ricky Belmonte, 54, Filipino actor, cerebral hemorrhage caused by a stroke.
  • Homer Elias, 46, American professional football player (Detroit Lions).[4]
  • Philip Goldson, 78, Belizean newspaper editor, activist and politician.
  • Gregorio Peralta, 66, Argentine boxer.

4[edit]

  • Blaise Alexander, 25, American race car driver, race crash.[5]
  • Patsy Burt, 73, British racing driver.
  • John Collins, 88, American jazz guitarist.
  • Arthur Daniels, 79, Welsh rugby league player.
  • Floyd Robert Gibson, 91, American judge (United States Circuit Judge of the United States Court of Appeals for the Eighth Circuit).[6]
  • Ahron Soloveichik, 84, American Torah scholar and rabbi.[7]

5[edit]

  • Peter Burge, 69, Australian cricketer.
  • Brian Edgar, 65, British rugby league player.
  • Woody Jensen, 94, American baseball player.[8]
  • Mike Mansfield, 98, American politician and diplomat (U.S. Representative from Montana, U.S. Senator from Montana, Senate Majority Leader).[9]
  • Robert Stevens, 63, American photo editor and anthrax attack victim.

6[edit]

  • Arne Harris, 67, American television producer-director (WGN-TV broadcasts of Chicago Cubs).[10]
  • Milton A. Rothman, 81, American nuclear physicist, complications due to diabetes.
  • Miguel del Toro, 29, Mexican baseball player.[11]

7[edit]

  • Christopher Adams, 46, English wrestler and judoka, brother of Olympic Judo star Neil Adams.
  • Herblock, 91, American editorial cartoonist (The Washington Post).[12]
  • Polly Rowles, 87, American actress (The Defenders, Sweet Liberty, Power).[13]

8[edit]

  • Kenneth L. Hale, 67, American linguist, known for study and preservation of endangered aboriginal languages.[14]
  • Seymour Heller, 87, American talent agent and manager (represented Liberace).[15]
  • Javed Iqbal, 45, Pakistani serial killer.

9[edit]

  • Roberto Campos, 84, Brazilian economist, writer, diplomat, and politician, heart attack.
  • Dagmar, 79, American actress, model, and television personality.
  • Herbert Ross, 74, American stage choreographer, and film director and producer (Funny Lady, The Turning Point).[16]
  • Károly Simonyi, 84, Hungarian physicist and writer.

10[edit]

  • Eddie Futch, 90, American boxing trainer (Joe Frazier, Ken Norton, Larry Holmes, Trevor Berbick).[17]
  • Luis Antonio García Navarro, 60, Spanish conductor (Music Director of the Teatro Real).[18]
  • Cal Gardner, 76, Canadian professional ice hockey player (New York Rangers, Toronto Maple Leafs, Chicago Black Hawks, Boston Bruins).[19]
  • Dave Gerard, 65, American baseball player.[20]
  • Vasily Mishin, 84, Soviet rocket designer.[21]

11[edit]

  • Tommy Harris, 76, English footballer.
  • Nada Mamula, 74, Yugoslavian sevdalinka singer.
  • Beni Montresor, 75, Italian artist, illustrator and set designer.
  • Thomas C. Wales, 49, American prosecutor and gun control advocate, murdered.

12[edit]

  • Richard Buckle, 85, British ballet critic.
  • Ruth Goetz, 89, American playwright (The Heiress) and screenwriter.[22]
  • Lord Hailsham of St Marylebone (Quintin Hogg), 94, British lawyer and politician.[23]
  • John T. Robinson, 78, South African palaeontologist.
  • Eddie Rodriguez, 69, Filipino film actor and director.
  • Hikmet Şimşek, Turkish orchestra conductor.
  • Otis Young, 69, American actor (The Outcasts, The Last Detail).[24]

13[edit]

  • Peter Doyle, 52, Australian pop singer (The New Seekers).[25]
  • Ubi Dwyer, 68, Irish anarchist.
  • Fritz Fromm, 88, German Olympic field handball player (gold medal winner of the men's team handball competition at the 1936 Summer Olympics).[26]
  • B. L. Graham, 87, American college basketball player and coach (Ole Miss).[27]
  • Glenn Johnson, 79, American professional football player (New York Yankees, Green Bay Packers, Winnipeg Blue Bombers).[28]
  • David Neil MacKenzie, 75, British linguist.

14[edit]

  • Sir Philip Adams, 85, British diplomat.
  • Willam Christensen, 99, American ballet dancer, choreographer and founder of the San Francisco Ballet and Ballet West in Salt Lake City, Utah.[29]
  • Eugene Grebenik, 82, British academic and demographer.[30]
  • Vernon Harrison, 89, British photographer and parapsychologist.
  • David Lewis, 60, American philosopher.
  • Ben Sankey, 94, American baseball player.[31]

15[edit]

  • Jamie Cann, 55, British Labour Party politician, liver disease.
  • Justus Cornelias Dirks, 90, South African author.
  • Anne Ridler, 89, British poet and editor.
  • Zhang Xueliang, 100, Chinese warlord and military figure.[32]

16[edit]

  • Gotthold Gloger, 77, German writer and painter.
  • Etta Jones, 72, American jazz singer, cancer.[33]
  • Yuri Ozerov, 80, Soviet film director and screenwriter.

17[edit]

  • Jay Livingston, 86, American composer (Academy Award for Best Original Song for "Buttons and Bows", "Mona Lisa" and "Que Sera, Sera").[34]
  • Micheline Ostermeyer, 78, French Olympic athlete (two gold and one bronze medal winner at the 1948 Summer Olympics) and concert pianist.[35]
  • Jack Smith, 77, American NASCAR driver, congestive heart failure.
  • Neil Tillotson, 102, American inventor.
  • Rehavam Ze'evi, 75, Israeli army general and politician.[36]

18[edit]

  • Emily Couric, 54, Virginia Democratic state senator, pancreatic cancer.
  • Ferris Fain, 80, American baseball player, complications from leukemia and diabetes.[37]
  • János Kulka, 80, Hungarian conductor and composer.

19[edit]

  • Kay Dick, 86, English journalist, novelist and autobiographer.[38]
  • Jagernath Lachmon, 85, Surinamese politician.[39]
  • Hugh Mulcahy, 88, American baseball player.[40]
  • Joe Murray, 80, American baseball player.[41]
  • Yang Jingren, 82/83, Chinese politician.

20[edit]

  • Frank Hodgkinson, 82, Australian painter and graphic artist.
  • Patricia Locke, 73, Native American educator-activist, heart-failure.
  • Kaviyoor Murali, Indian activist.
  • Nebojša Popović, 78, Serbian basketball player and coach.
  • John H. Terry, 76, American lawyer and politician.
  • Andrew Waterhouse, 42, English poet and musician, suicide.

21[edit]

  • George Feyer, 92, Hungarian-American cafe pianist and entertainer.[42]
  • Margaret Hope MacPherson, 93, Scottish crofter, politician, author, and activist.
  • Sir John Plumb, 90, British historian.

22[edit]

  • Howard Finster, 84, American artist and Baptist minister.
  • Ernest Hilgard, 97, American psychologist and professor at Stanford University.[43]
  • Bertie Mee, 82, English footballer.
  • Ed Vijent, 38, Dutch footballer, stabbed.
  • Georgy Vitsin, 84, Soviet and Russian actor.

23[edit]

  • Ken Aston, 86, British football referee.
  • Josh Kirby, 72, British artist.[44]
  • Linden Travers, 88, British actress (The Lady Vanishes, No Orchids for Miss Blandish).[45]
  • Daniel Wildenstein, 84, French art dealer (Wildenstein & Co.), historian and owner-breeder of thoroughbred race horses.[46]

24[edit]

  • Kathleen Ankers, 82, American theatrical and television set and costume designer (Late Night with David Letterman, The Rosie O'Donnell Show).[47]
  • Kim Gardner, 53, English musician (Badger, Ashton, Gardner & Dyke, The Birds, The Creation).[48]
  • Eugenio Granell, 88, Spanish painter (often described as "the last Spanish surrealist painter").[49]
  • Jaromil Jireš, 65, Czechoslovak filmmaker.[50]
  • Eamon Kelly, 87, Irish actor.
  • Bill Mueller, 80, American baseball player.[51]

25[edit]

  • Jack Blackwell, 91, English footballer.
  • Marvin Harris, 74, American anthropologist.
  • Yoritsune Matsudaira, 94, Japanese composer.

26[edit]

  • Charlie van Gelderen, 88, South African journalist and Trotskyist, and the last surviving participant of Leon Trotsky's 4th International in 1938.[52]
  • Laszlo Halasz, 96, Hungarian-American music director (New York City Opera).[53]
  • Abdul Haq, 43, Afghan mujahideen commander, executed by the Taliban.[54]
  • Eugene Jackson, 84, American child actor who was a regular of the Our Gang series (The Big Town, Shootin' Injuns, Little Annie Rooney, The Addams Family).[55]
  • Elizabeth Jennings, 75, English poet.[56]
  • John Platts-Mills, 95, British politician and lawyer.

27[edit]

  • Fr. Abel, 81, Indian Catholic CMI priest, cardiac arrest.
  • Maragatham Chandrasekar, 84, Indian politician and Member of Parliament.
  • Dirk Willem van Krevelen, 86, Dutch chemical engineer.
  • Pradeep Kumar, 76, Indian actor.
  • John P. Roberts, 56, American businessman, promoter of the Woodstock Festival.

28[edit]

  • Jallouli Fares, 92, Tunisian politician.
  • Gerard Hengeveld, 90, Dutch pianist, composer and educator.[57]
  • Hans Hohenester, 84, German Olympic bobsledder (two-man bobsleigh at the 1956 Winter Olympics).[58]
  • Sir John Mogg, 88, British army general.
  • Richard Halsey Best, 91, World War two bomber pilot

29[edit]

  • Maura Fay, 43, Australian casting director.
  • Angelo Ippolito, 78, Italian-American painter (Museum of Modern Art, Whitney Museum of American Art, Metropolitan Museum of Art).[59]
  • Freddie Silva, 63, Sri Lankan actor, singer.

30[edit]

  • David Cochran, 86, American bishop of the Episcopal Diocese of Alaska.
  • Marga Legal, 93, German actress.
  • Johnny Lucadello, 82, American baseball player.[60]
  • William R. Smallwood, 73, Canadian politician.

31[edit]

  • Colin Campbell, 59, Canadian video artist.
  • Régine Cavagnoud, 31, French Olympic and World Cup alpine ski racer (2001 World Champion in Super G).[61]
  • Yutaka Fujimoto, 50, Japanese Olympic basketball player (men's basketball at the 1976 Summer Olympics).[62]
  • Warren Elliot Henry, 92, American physicist, made significant contributions to radar technology and the physical properties of materials.[63]
  • Angus MacVicar, 93, British author.
  • Braj Kumar Nehru, 90, Indian diplomat and ambassador.
  • Art Wall Jr., 77, American professional golfer (1959 winner of the Masters Tournament).[64]
