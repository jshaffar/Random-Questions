The following is a list of notable deaths in December 2002.

Entries for each day are listed alphabetically by surname. A typical entry lists information in the following sequence:

  • Name, age, country of citizenship at birth, subsequent country of citizenship (if applicable), reason for notability, cause of death (if known), and reference.

December 2002[edit]

1[edit]

  • Edward L. Beach Jr., 84, American highly decorated United States Navy submarine officer and best-selling author (Run Silent, Run Deep).[1]
  • Omar Blebel, 80, Argentine Olympic wrestler (1948 men's Greco-Roman featherweight, 1952 men's freestyle bantamweight).[2]
  • C. Chapin Cutler, 87, American communications engineer, known for inventions in radio, radar, signal coding, imaging and satellite communications.[3]
  • Harry Dick, 82, Canadian professional ice hockey player (Chicago Blackhawks).[4]
  • Dave McNally, 60, American baseball player (Baltimore Orioles, Montreal Expos), lung cancer.[5]
  • José Chávez Morado, 93, Mexican artist.
  • Michael Oliver, 65, British classical music broadcaster and writer.

2[edit]

  • Elizabeth B. Andrews, 91, American politician (U.S. Representative for Alabama's 3rd congressional district).[6]
  • Sanford Soverhill Atwood, 89, American scientist, plant cytologist and president of Emory University.[7]
  • Barney Berlinger, 94, American Olympic decathlon athlete (men's decathlon in the 1928 Summer Olympics).[8]
  • Aileen Fisher, 96, American writer of more than 100 children's books (The Coffee-Pot Face, Runny Days, Sunny Days).[9]
  • Jim Mitchell, 56, Irish politician.
  • Vjenceslav Richter, 85, Croatian architect.
  • Derek Robinson, 61, British nuclear physicist.
  • Ben Wade, 80, American baseball player (Chicago Cubs, Brooklyn Dodgers, St. Louis Cardinals, Pittsburgh Pirates) and scout.[10]
  • Mal Waldron, 77, American jazz pianist and composer (Billie Holiday, Charles Mingus, John Coltrane).[11]
  • Fay Gillis Wells, 94, American pioneer aviator.
  • David Whiffen, 80, English physical chemist, known for his work on infrared spectroscopy and electron spin resonance spectroscopy.[12]
  • Fred Zain, 51, American forensic laboratory technician, falsified results to obtain convictions, liver cancer.[13]

3[edit]

  • Hariharananda Giri, 95, Indian yogi and guru.
  • Pierre Guillaume, 77, officer of the French Navy.
  • Christian Liger, 67, French writer.
  • Emanuel Papper, 87, American anesthesiologist, professor, and author.
  • Glenn Quinn, 32, Irish actor (Roseanne, Angel), heroin overdose.
  • Jug Thesenga, 88, American baseball player (Washington Senators).[14]

4[edit]

  • Charles Pierce Davey, 77, American welterweight boxer, complications from paralysis.[15]
  • Habibullah, Afghan US detainee, homicide.
  • Dolores Renze, 95, American archivist and administrator.
  • Hemmo Silvennoinen, 70, Finnish ski jumper.

5[edit]

  • Roone Arledge, 71, American television producer and executive (Monday Night Football, Nightline).[16]
  • Bob Berg, 51, American jazz saxophonist (Miles Davis, Chick Corea), traffic accident.[17]
  • Prosper Boulanger, 84, Canadian politician and a member of Parliament (House of Commons representing Mercier, Quebec).[18]
  • Brigitte Massin, 75, French musicologist and journalist.
  • Ne Win, 91, Burmese dictator.
  • Jackie Walker, 52, American football player, complications from AIDS.
  • Ann Welch, 85, British glider pilot (gliding, hang gliding, paragliding, microlight flying).[19]
  • Pedro d'aguillon 87, Mexican Actor Mexico City, Mexico

6[edit]

  • Jerzy Adamski, 65, Polish featherweight boxer (silver medal winner in featherweight boxing at the 1960 Summer Olympics).[20]
  • Clarence Beers, 83, American baseball player (St. Louis Cardinals).[21]
  • Father Philip Berrigan, 79, American priest and political activist.[22]
  • Russell Blattner, 94, American pediatrician and founding physician-in-chief of Texas Children's Hospital.[23]
  • Leroy M. Zimmerman, 69, American politician.

7[edit]

  • R. Orin Cornett, 89, American physicist, university professor and inventor of Cued Speech.[24]
  • John R. Dellenback, 84, American politician (U.S. Representative for Oregon's 4th congressional district), viral pneumonia.[25]
  • Clare Deniz, 91, British jazz pianist.[26]
  • Barbara Howard, 76, Canadian painter, wood engraver, bookbinder and designer, pulmonary embolism.
  • Paddy Tunney, 81, Irish traditional artist.

8[edit]

  • Gunnar Helén, 84, Swedish politician.
  • Bobby Joe Hill, 59, American basketball player.
  • Charles Rosen, 85, American computer scientist.
  • Dorothy Walker, 73, Irish art critic and historian, active in the development of the Irish Museum of Modern Art.[27]

9[edit]

  • Shigeru Chiba, 83, Japanese baseball player and manager, perhaps the greatest second baseman in Japanese baseball history.[28]
  • Johnny Lazor, 90, American baseball player (Boston Red Sox).[29]
  • Stan Rice, 60, painter, educator, poet, husband of author Anne Rice, cancer.
  • To Huu, 82, Vietnamese poet and politician.

10[edit]

  • Desmond Keith Carter, 35, convicted murderer, executed by lethal injection in North Carolina.
  • Warwick Charlton, 84, English journalist, prime mover behind construction and sailing of Mayflower II from the U.K. to the U.S.[30]
  • Les Costello, 74, Canadian professional ice hockey player and Catholic priest (Toronto Maple Leafs).[31]
  • Earl Henry, 85, American baseball player (Cleveland Indians).[32]
  • Mike Kosman, 85, American baseball player (Cincinnati Reds).[33]
  • Andres Küng, 57, Swedish journalist, writer, entrepreneur and politician of Estonian origin.
  • Steve Llewellyn, 78, Welsh rugby league player.
  • Ian MacNaughton, 76, Scottish director of most episodes of Monty Python's Flying Circus.
  • Homer Spragins, 82, American baseball player (Philadelphia Phillies).[34]

11[edit]

  • Dolly Dawn, 86, American big band vocalist and recording star of the 1930s and 1940s.[35]
  • Muzaffer Demirhan, 70, Turkish alpine skier (Winter Olympics: 1948, 1956, 1960, 1964).[36]
  • Bob Loane, 88, American baseball player (Washington Senators, Boston Bees).[37]
  • Arthur Metcalfe, 64, British racing cyclist, cancer.
  • Nanabhoy Palkhivala, 82, Indian jurist and economist.
  • Marvin Breckinridge Patterson, 97, American photojournalist, cinematographer, and philanthropist.
  • Kay Rose, 80, American Oscar-winning sound editor.

12[edit]

  • Dee Brown, 94, American author and historian (Bury My Heart at Wounded Knee).[38]
  • Nancy Caroline, 58, American EMS physician and writer (Emergency Care in the Streets).[39]
  • Brad Dexter, 85, American actor and film producer (Run Silent Run Deep, The Magnificent Seven, None but the Brave).[40]
  • Edward Harrison, 92, English cricketer and squash player.
  • Jay Wesley Neill, 37, convicted murderer, executed by lethal injection in Oklahoma.

13[edit]

  • Maria Björnson, 53, French theatre designer, two-time Tony Award winner for The Phantom of the Opera (Best Scenic Design, Best Costume Design).[41]
  • Stella Brooks, 92, American jazz singer of the 1940s.[42]
  • Ronald Butt, 82, British journalist, wrote a political column for The Times and was the author of two books on Parliament.[43]
  • Zal Yanovsky, 57, Canadian folk rock musician, lead guitarist and singer for The Lovin' Spoonful.[44]
  • Lucien Zins, 80, French Olympic swimmer (men's 100 metre backstroke: 1948, 1952).[45]

14[edit]

  • Hank Arft, 80, American baseball player (St. Louis Browns).[46]
  • Jack Bradley, 86, English footballer.
  • Ray Wietecha, 74, American professional football player (Michigan State, New York Giants) and coach.[47]
  • Antoni Woryna, 61, Polish speedway rider.

15[edit]

  • John Crosby, 76, American conductor, founded the Santa Fe Opera.[48]
  • Charles E. Fraser, 73, American real estate developer, transformed Hilton Head Island into a world-class resort.[49]
  • Vladimir Haensel, 88, American chemical engineer.
  • Arthur Jeph Parker, 79, American set decorator.
  • Dick Stuart, 70, American baseball player (Pittsburgh Pirates, Boston Red Sox, Philadelphia Phillies).[50]

16[edit]

  • Bill Hunter, 82, Canadian ice hockey player, general manager and coach, cancer.
  • Rolston James, 26, Trinidadian international football player, homicide.
  • Don Vesco, 63, American businessperson and motorcycle racer, prostate cancer.

17[edit]

  • Colin Clark, 70, British film director and writer (My Week with Marilyn).[51]
  • John Aubrey Davis, Sr., 90, American civil rights activist.[52]
  • Mahmoud Fayad, 77, Egyptian weightlifter (gold medal in featherweight weightlifting at the 1948 Summer Olympics).[53]
  • Aideu Handique, 87, Indian actress.
  • Hank Luisetti, 86, American basketball star and innovator.
  • Dame Mona Mitchell, 64, British courtier.

18[edit]

  • Saul Amarel, 74, American computer scientist, known for his pioneering work in artificial intelligence.[54]
  • Earl Audet, 81, American professional football player (USC, Washington Redskins, Los Angeles Dons) and actor.[55]
  • Lucy Grealy, 39, Irish-born American poet and memoirist.
  • Ramon John Hnatyshyn, 68, former Governor-General of Canada, pancreatitis.
  • Sir Bert Millichip, 88, British football administrator.
  • Wayne Owens, 65, U.S. Congressman (D-UT), heart attack.[56]

19[edit]

  • Guy Bordelon, 80, American Korean War flying ace.
  • Claude Crocker, 78, American baseball player (Brooklyn Dodgers).[57]
  • Stephen Fleck, 90, American psychiatrist.
  • Jim Flower, 79, British admiral.
  • Robert Evan Kendell, 67, Welsh psychiatrist.
  • Bob Rinker, 81, American baseball player (Philadelphia Athletics).[58]
  • Arthur Rowley, 76, English footballer, holder of the record for most career league goals scored.
  • Lewis B. Smedes, 81, American theologian.
  • Roger Webb, 68, British musical director and composer (The Godsend, The Boy in Blue, Death of a Centerfold).[59]
  • George Weller, 95, American World War II journalist, his Nagasaki nuclear bomb blast stories censored by U.S. military.[60]

20[edit]

  • Leonard Bishop, 80, American novelist, and newspaper columnist.
  • Joanne Campbell, 38, British actress, starred in the 1980s comedy series Me and My Girl, deep-vein thrombosis.[61]
  • Robert "Sonny" Carson, 66, U.S. Army Korean War veteran and civil rights activist.[62]
  • James Richard Ham, 91, American Roman Catholic prelate.
  • John W. Hicks, 81, American agricultural economist and academic administrator.
  • Wheeler J. North, 80, American marine biologist and environmental scientist.
  • Grote Reber, 90, American pioneer of radio astronomy.

  • José Hierro, 80, Spanish poet.
  • Glen Seator, 46, American visual artist and conceptual sculptor, accidental fall.
  • Victor Watts, 64, British toponymist, medievalist, translator, and academic, heart attack.

  • V.K. Ramasamy, 76, Indian actor.
  • Jake Thackray, 64, English singer-songwriter, heart failure.
  • Arch Wilder, 85, Canadian professional hockey player (Detroit Red Wings).[76]

  • Gabriel Almond, 91, American political scientist.[77]
  • William T. Orr, 85, American television executive producer (Maverick, F-Troop, 77 Sunset Strip).[78]
  • Davina Whitehouse, 90, British-born New Zealand actress (Night Nurse, Sleeping Dogs, Braindead).[79]

26[edit]

  • Herb Ritts, 50, celebrity photographer.
  • Armand Zildjian, 81, Armenian-American manufacturer of cymbals, chairman of the Avedis Zildjian Company.[82]

27[edit]

  • Truid Blaisse-Terwindt, 85, Dutch hockey- and tennis player.
  • Bill Chipley, 82, American professional football player (Boston Yanks, New York Bulldogs).[83]
  • Carla Henius, 83, German soprano and mezzo-soprano and librettist.
  • George Roy Hill, 81, American film director (Butch Cassidy and the Sundance Kid, The Sting).[84]
  • Mallory Evan Wijesinghe, 84, Sri Lankan engineer and entrepreneur.
  • Matsui Yayori, 68, Japanese journalist and women's rights activist.

28[edit]

  • Baba Raúl Cañizares, 47, American Santerían priest and writer.
  • Maria Carbone, 94, Italian operatic soprano.
  • Vladimir Chuyan, 62, Soviet sports shooter (men's 50 metre rifle three positions, men's 50 metre rifle prone at the 1964 Summer Olympics).[85]
  • Koreyoshi Kurahara, 75, Japanese screenwriter and director.
  • Albert Stubbins, 83, English footballer.
  • Joseph Vadakkan, 83, Indian political activist priest and freedom fighter.
  • Meri Wilson, 53, American model and singer-songwriter ("Telephone Man"), car accident.[86]

29[edit]

  • Al Babartsky, 87, American professional football player (Fordham University, Chicago Cardinals, Chicago Bears).[87]
  • Billy Brown, 84, American triple jumper and long jumper (1941 world long jump leader, 1936 Summer Olympics men's triple jump).[88]
  • Don Clarke, 69, New Zealand rugby player.
  • John Dreyfus, 84, British typographer and printing historian, his writings investigated and celebrated the evolution of type.[89]
  • Sir Paul Hawkins, 90, British politician.
  • Victoria Lederberg, 65, Justice of the Rhode Island Supreme Court (1993–2002).
  • Július Satinský, 61, Slovak actor and comedian.

  • Antony Ponzini, 69, American actor.
