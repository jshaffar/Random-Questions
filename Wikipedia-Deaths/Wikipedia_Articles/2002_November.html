The following is a list of notable deaths in November 2002.

Entries for each day are listed alphabetically by surname. A typical entry lists information in the following sequence:

  • Name, age, country of citizenship at birth, subsequent country of citizenship (if applicable), reason for notability, cause of death (if known), and reference.

November 2002[edit]

1[edit]

  • Ekrem Akurgal, 91, Turkish archaeologist.
  • Yisrael Amir, 99, Israeli Air Force commander.
  • David Bartleet, 73, British Anglican prelate, Bishop of Tonbridge.
  • Edward Brooke, 85, Canadian Olympic fencer (1952 Summer Olympics: men's foil, men's épée).[1]
  • Nicholas John Bua, 77, American judge (U.S. District Judge of the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Illinois).[2]
  • Amadou Cissé Dia, 87, Senegalese politician and playwright.
  • Käte Jaenicke, 79, German theater and film actress.
  • Lester Morgan, 26, Costa Rican professional football goalkeeper, suicide.
  • Keith A. Wester, 62, American film sound engineer, Emmy Award winner and six time Academy Awards nominee.[3]
  • Sir Charles Wilson, 93, British political scientist.

2[edit]

  • Brian Behan, 75, Irish writer and playwright, younger brother of Brendan Behan.[4]
  • Robert Haslam, Baron Haslam, 79, British industrialist and life peer.
  • Lo Lieh, 63, Hong Kong actor.
  • Dame Felicity Peake, 89, British Director of the Women's Royal Air Force.
  • Tonio Selwart, 106, Bavarian actor and Broadway performer.
  • Charles Sheffield, 67, English-born American science fiction author and physicist.
  • Richard F. Visotcky, 73, American politician, member of the New Jersey General Assembly.

3[edit]

  • Mary Bird, 92, American Olympic alpine skier (women's combined alpine skiing at the 1936 Summer Olympics).[5]
  • Lonnie Donegan, 71, British skiffle musician ("Rock Island Line", "John Henry").[6]
  • Sir John Habakkuk, 87, British economic historian.
  • Jonathan Harris, 87, American actor, TV's "Dr. Smith" on Lost in Space.
  • William Packard, 69, American poet and author.
  • Sir Rex Roe, 77, British air force officer.

4[edit]

  • Antonio Margheriti, 72, Italian filmmaker, heart attack.
  • Xing Qiyi, 90, Chinese chemist.
  • Malcolm Ross, 91, Canadian literary critic, pneumonia.
  • Ross Wilson, 83, Canadian ice hockey trainer (Detroit Red Wings) and backup goalie.[7]

5[edit]

  • Vinnette Justine Carroll, 80, American Broadway director (Don't Bother Me, I Can't Cope, Your Arms Too Short to Box with God).[8]
  • Ansley J. Coale, 84, American demographer, senior research demographer at the Office of Population Research at Princeton.[9]
  • Marcel Dheere, 83, Canadian professional ice hockey player (Montreal Canadiens).[10]
  • Billy Guy, 66, American singer.
  • Mushtaq Qadri, 35, Pakistani religious poet.
  • Arthur Winfree, 60, American theoretical biologist, known for his studies of biological oscillations.[11]

6[edit]

  • Folke Frölén, 94, Swedish Olympic equestrian (equestrian at the 1952 Summer Olympics).[12]
  • Brian James, 61, English cricketer.
  • Sid Sackson, 82, board game designer.
  • Gianluca Signorini, 42, Italian footballer, amyotrophic lateral sclerosis.

7[edit]

  • Rudolf Augstein, 79, German journalist and publisher, founder and chief editorialist of Der Spiegel.[13]
  • Charles Hambro, Baron Hambro, 72, British merchant banker and political fundraiser.
  • Dilys Hamlett, 74, British actress.
  • Peg Phillips, 84, American actress, pulmonary disease.
  • Tom Reynolds, 84, Australian rules footballer.
  • Pedro Juan Soto, 74, Puerto Rican writer, killed by police officers.

8[edit]

  • Tom Barrington, 58, American professional football player (Ohio State, Washington Redskins, New Orleans Saints).[14]
  • Norma Lee Clark, 75, American actress (Captain Video and His Video Rangers), author and personal assistant to Woody Allen.[15]
  • Jaun Elia, 70, Pakistani Marxist.
  • Ivan Kandyba, 72, Ukrainian lawyer and dissident.
  • Ke Zhao, 92, Chinese mathematician.
  • Franjo Kukuljević, 93, Croatian tennis player.
  • Dorothy Mackie Low, 86, British novelist under pseudonyms Dorothy Mackie Low, Lois Paxton, and Zoë Cass.
  • Querube Makalintal, 91, Filipino Chief Justice of the Supreme Court.
  • Zoé Oldenbourg, 86, Russian-born French historian and novelist.
  • Christopher Parsons, 70, English wildlife film-maker producer.
  • Donald Niven Wheeler, 89, American social activist, teacher, and alleged Soviet spy.

9[edit]

  • Bill Baxter, 78, Scottish footballer.
  • Dick Johnson, 85, American test pilot.
  • Cliff Patton, 79, American professional football player (Philadelphia Eagles, Chicago Cardinals).[16]
  • Merlin Santana, 26, American actor, shot.
  • William Schutz, 76, American psychologist.
  • Eusebio Tejera, 80, Uruguyan footballer.

10[edit]

  • Michel Boisrond, 81, French film director and screenwriter, known for directing Brigitte Bardot in Naughty Girl.[17]
  • Steve Durbano, 50, Canadian ice hockey player, known for his rough play and larger-than-life persona (Pittsburgh Penguins, St. Louis Blues), lung cancer.[18]
  • Franco Fantasia, 78, Italian actor.
  • Ron Jacobs, 74, English rugby union player.
  • Émile Ollivier, 62, Haitian-born Canadian writer.
  • Ken Raffensberger, 85, American baseball player (St. Louis Cardinals, Chicago Cubs, Philadelphia Phillies, Cincinnati Reds/Redlegs).[19]
  • Gert Westphal, 82, German-Swiss actor, recitator and director.

11[edit]

  • Frances Ames, 82, South African neurologist, psychiatrist, and human rights activist, leukemia.
  • Sir Michael Clapham, 90, British industrialist, president of the Confederation of British Industry from 1972 to 1974.[20]
  • Mary Hamilton, 67, African-American civil rights activist, ovarian cancer.
  • Bernard J. Liska, 71, American food scientist.
  • Esther Raziel-Naor, 90, Israeli Zionist, Irgun leader and politician.
  • Esther Somerfeld-Ziskind, 101, American neurologist and psychiatrist.
  • David Steel, 92, Scottish minister.
  • Marcia Van Dyke, 80, American violinist and actress.
  • Yoo Youngkuk, 86, Korean abstract artist.

12[edit]

  • Wally Barron, 90, American politician (26th governor of West Virginia), indicted and plead guilty to jury tampering.[21]
  • David Francis Clyde, 77, British tropical physician, known for his research on malaria vaccines and chemotherapy.[22]
  • Glenn Dobbs, 82, American professional football player (Brooklyn Dodgers, Los Angeles Dons) and college football coach (University of Tulsa).[23]
  • Johannes Kerkorrel, 42, South African singer-songwriter, journalist and playwright, suicide by hanging.

13[edit]

  • Frederick Valentine Atkinson, 86, British mathematician (Atkinson's theorem, Atkinson–Wilcox theorem).[24]
  • Bill Berry, 72, American jazz trumpeter (Duke Ellington Orchestra, Bill Berry and the L.A. Band).[25]
  • Tom Caldwell, 81, Northern Ireland politician, unionist and interior designer, member of Parliament of Northern Ireland representing Belfast Willowfield.[26]
  • Edwin Ferdon, 89, American ethnologist, known for his field work in Ecuador, Mexico, the South Pacific and the U.S. Southwest.[27]
  • Roland Hanna, 70, American jazz pianist, composer, and teacher, viral infection.
  • Desmond Norman, 73, aircraft designer and aviation pioneer, heart attack.
  • Kaloji Narayana Rao, 88, Indian poet, freedom fighter, and political activist.
  • Irv Rubin, 57, Canadian chairman of the Jewish Defence League.
  • Juan Alberto Schiaffino, 77, Italian-Uruguayan football player.

14[edit]

  • Eddie Bracken, 87, American actor (Hail the Conquering Hero, The Miracle of Morgan's Creek, National Lampoon's Vacation).[28]
  • Walter Crocker, 100, Australian diplomat, writer and war veteran.[29]
  • James R. Hendrix, 77, US Army sergeant and a recipient of the Medal of Honor, cancer.
  • Gourish Kaikini, 90, Indian litterateur, teacher and columnist.
  • Rosemary Forbes Kerry, 89, American nurse and social activist.
  • Gedong Bagus Oka, 81, Indonesian Hindu reformer and philosopher.[30]
  • Dale E. Saffels, 81, American lawyer, legislator, and District Judge.
  • Mir Qazi, 38, Pakistani convicted criminal, executed by lethal injection in Virginia.

15[edit]

  • Ed Freed, 83, American baseball player (Philadelphia Phillies).[31]
  • Roberta Leighton, 70, American drag racer.
  • JJ Stewart, 79, New Zealand rugby coach.
  • Earl L. Warrick, 91, American chemist, though disputed, claimed to be the inventor of Silly Putty.[32]

16[edit]

  • Ramli Ahmad, 46, Malaysian Olympic sprinter (1976 Summer Olympics: men's 100 metres, men's 200 metres).[33]
  • George Barrie, 90, American businessman (owner and CEO of Fabergé Inc.) and songwriter (two-time nominee for Academy Award for Best Original Song).[34]
  • Rupert E. Billingham, 81, British biologist, considered by many to have founded the fields of reproductive immunology and organ transplantation.[35]
  • Tom Farris, 82, American professional football player (University of Wisconsin, Chicago Bears, Chicago Rockets).[36]
  • Sir George Gardiner, 67, British politician.
  • Alfred Lewis Levitt, 86, American film and television screenwriter, heart failure.
  • Frank Smithies, 90, British mathematician.
  • Mose Vinson, 85, American boogie-woogie, blues and jazz pianist and singer, diabetes.

17[edit]

  • Maria Bogner, German fashion designer.
  • Robert Brattain, 91, American physicist.
  • Abba Eban, 88, Israeli Foreign Affairs Minister, ambassador to the U.S., ambassador to the U.N.[37]
  • Frank McCarthy, 78, American artist and painter, lung cancer.
  • Marvin Mirisch, 84, American film producer, cancer.

18[edit]

  • Angus Cameron, 93, American book editor and publisher, known for being blacklisted during McCarthy era.[38]
  • James Coburn, 74,American actor and Oscar-winner, heart attack.[39]
  • Francesco De Martino, 95, Italian jurist and politician, considered to be the conscience of the Italian Socialist Party.[40]
  • Kim Gallagher, 38, American middle-distance runner, stroke.
  • Peter Grippe, 90, American sculptor, printmaker, and painter.
  • Edith Hirsch Luchins, 80, Polish-American mathematician.
  • Pete Orr, 46, American stock car racing driver, cancer.
  • Pasquale Vivolo, 74, Italian footballer.
  • Juliusz Wyrzykowski, 56, Polish movie and stage actor.

19[edit]

  • John Bunting, 75, English sculptor and teacher.[41]
  • Vito Ciancimino, 78, Italian politician (mayor of Palermo, Sicily) and Mafia member, made a fortune from bribery and embezzlement.[42]
  • George Fullerton, 79, South African cricketer.
  • Prince Alexandre de Merode, 68, Belgian International Olympic Committee member, lung cancer.
  • Jean-Claude Renard, 80, French poet.
  • Harry Watson, 79, Canadian professional hockey player (Detroit Red Wings, Toronto Maple Leafs, Chicago Black Hawks).[43]

20[edit]

  • Nina Elias-Bamberger, 48, American producer of children's television (Dragon Tales, Big Bag, 3-2-1 Contact, Big Bird in China, Tiny Planets).[44]
  • George Guest, 78, British organist and choirmaster.
  • Ben Webb, 45, Canadian journalist, editor of Sanity, the Campaign for Nuclear Disarmament.[45]
  • Margita White, 65, American White House press official under Richard M. Nixon and Gerald R. Ford.[46]
  • Zhang Shuguang, 82, Chinese politician.

21[edit]

  • Robert Brentano, 76, American history professor, prize-winning author and historian of medieval England and Italy.[47]
  • Hadda Brooks, 86, American jazz singer, pianist and composer, known as the "Queen of the Boogie".[48]
  • Amílcar de Castro, 82, Brazilian sculptor, known for works in iron.[49]
  • George Emslie, Baron Emslie, 82, Scottish judge and life peer.[50]
  • Arturo Guzman Decena founder of Los Zetas.
  • J. Roger Pichette, 81, Canadian politician.
  • Prince Takamado, 47, Japanese prince.

22[edit]

  • Parley Baer, 88, American radio, television and film actor (The Andy Griffith Show, The Adventures of Ozzie and Harriet, The Addams Family, Dave).[51]
  • Joan Barclay, 88, American actress.
  • Norman Clarke, 86, British physicist and politician.
  • Christine Marion Fraser, 64, Scottish novelist.[52]
  • Arne Mellnäs, 69, Swedish composer.

23[edit]

  • Erna Bogen-Bogáti, 95, Hungarian Olympic fencer (1928 women's foil, 1932 bronze medal women's foil, 1936 women's foil).[53]
  • Boudewijn Büch, 53, Dutch writer, poet and television presenter.[54]
  • Maritie Carpentier, 79, French television show producer.
  • Roberto Matta, 91 Chilean artist.
  • Billy Travis, 41, American professional wrestler.

24[edit]

  • Branko Dangubić, 80, Yugoslavian (Serbian) Olympic javelin thrower (men's javelin throw at the 1952 Summer Olympics).[55]
  • Noel Davis, 75, English film and television actor and casting director (Merlin, Reds).[56]
  • Mikhail Devyatayev, 85, Soviet fighter pilot who escaped from a Nazi concentration camp.[57]
  • Harriet Doerr, 92, American author.[58]
  • Cecil Dowdy, 57, American college football player (University of Alabama) and businessman, hunting accident.[59]
  • Kazım Ergin, 87, Turkish geophysicist.
  • Lewis Samuel Feuer, 89, American sociologist, philosopher, professor and author.[60]
  • Philip B. Meggs, 60, American graphic designer.
  • John Rawls, 81, American moral and political philosopher.
  • Baba Sidhaye, 70, Indian cricketer.
  • Sidney S. Wade, 93, American major general in the U.S. Marine Corps.[61]

25[edit]

  • Ed Bliss, 90, American broadcast journalist, news editor and educator (Edward R. Murrow, Walter Cronkite).[62]
  • Charles E. Chamberlain, 85, American politician (U.S. Representative for Michigan's 6th congressional district).[63]
  • Gordon Davidson, 87, Australian politician (member of Australian Senate representing South Australia).[64]
  • David Drummond, 8th Earl of Perth, 95, British politician and aristocrat.
  • Karel Reisz, 76, British filmmaker.
  • Eugene V. Rostow, 89, American legal scholar and public servant.

26[edit]

  • Frank Allaun, 89, British politician (member of Parliament for Salford East from 1955 to 1983).[65]
  • Jim Butterfield, 74, American head football coach at Ithaca College (three NCAA Division III Football Championships).[66]
  • Ruzha Delcheva, 87, Bulgarian actress.
  • Ralph Engelstad, 72, American casino executive (Imperial Palace).[67]
  • Raymond Gafner, 87, Swiss ice hockey player and referee.
  • Ernest Leiser, 81, American television producer.
  • Isabel McLaughlin, 99, Canadian painter and philanthropist.
  • Raymond L. Wallace, 84, American Bigfoot hoaxer.[68]
  • Verne Winchell, 87, American business executive, founder of Winchell's Donuts, president and CEO of Denny's.[69]

27[edit]

  • Billie Bird, 94, American actress (Sixteen Candles, Ernest Saves Christmas, Home Alone, Dennis the Menace).[70]
  • Stanley Black, 89, English bandleader, composer, conductor and pianist.[71]
  • Laurence J. Burton, 76, American politician (U.S. Representative for Utah's 1st congressional district).[72]
  • George Christian, 75, American journalist, White House press secretary for President Lyndon B. Johnson.[73]
  • Ronald Gerard Connors, 87, American Roman Catholic bishop in the Dominican Republic.
  • Bob deLauer, 82, American professional football player (USC, Cleveland/Los Angeles Rams).[74]
  • Edwin L. Mechem, 90, American politician.
  • Yeruham Meshel, 90, Israeli union leader and politician.
  • Robert W. Straub, 82, American politician, Alzheimer's disease.
  • Shivmangal Singh Suman, 87, Indian poet, heart attack.

28[edit]

  • Mahicon Librelato, 21, Brazilian footballer, traffic accident.
  • Russell Arthur Missin, 80, British organist.
  • Lennaert Nijgh, 57, Dutch lyricist, gastrointestinal bleeding.
  • Billy Pearson, American jockey, film actor, and an art dealer.
  • Dave "Snaker" Ray, 59, American blues singer and guitarist, lung cancer.

29[edit]

  • Melih Cevdet Anday, 87, Turkish author.
  • Damien Covington, 29, American professional football player (Buffalo Bills), killed in an attempted robbery.[75]
  • Saburō Ienaga, 89, Japanese historian.
  • John Justin, 85, British stage and film actor.
  • David Weiss, 93, American novelist (Naked Came I).[76]

30[edit]

  • Alan Ashman, 74, English footballer player.
  • Minuetta Kessler, 88, Russian-born American concert pianist and composer.
  • Bill Sparks, 80, British Royal Marine Commando in World War II.
  • Tim Woods, 68, professional wrestler who wrestled as Mr. Wrestling, heart attack.
