The following is a list of notable deaths in June 2006.

Entries for each day are listed alphabetically by surname. A typical entry lists information in the following sequence:

  • Name, age, country of citizenship at birth, subsequent country of citizenship (if applicable), reason for notability, cause of death (if known), and reference.

June 2006[edit]

1[edit]

  • Radu Bălescu, 73, Romanian scientist.[1]
  • Shokichi Iyanaga, 100, Japanese mathematician.[2]
  • Rocío Jurado, 61, Spanish singer and actress, pancreatic cancer.[3]
  • Allan Prior, 84, British television scriptwriter (Z-Cars, Howards' Way, The Charmer), father of folk singer Maddy Prior.[4]
  • Abdul Latif Sharif, 59, Egyptian chemist, suspect in the femicides in Ciudad Juárez, Mexico, officially of natural causes, rumored poisoning.
  • Jack Shelton, 82, Australian cricketer.[5]
  • William D. Winn, 59, American professor of education at the University of Washington.[6]

2[edit]

  • Ronald Cass, 83, British film score composer.[7]
  • Roy Farran, 85, British army officer.[8]
  • Bernard Loomis, 82, American toymaker responsible for Strawberry Shortcake and Star Wars action figures, heart disease.[9]
  • Leon Pownall, 63, Canadian actor, cancer.[10]
  • Vince Welnick, 55, American keyboardist, member of The Grateful Dead, suicide.[11]
  • Edward Yates, 87, American television director, director of American Bandstand (1952–1969).[12]
  • Vyacheslav Klykov, 66, Russian sculptor and nationalist politician.[13]

3[edit]

  • Leo Clarke, 82, Australian Roman Catholic Bishop of Maitland–Newcastle, Australia, 1976-1995.[14]
  • Brian Duke, 79, Ugandan-born tropical disease expert who helped to save millions from river blindness.[15]
  • Johnny Grande, 76, American pianist, member of Bill Haley's backing band, The Comets. Complications arising from cancer.[16]
  • George Kashdan, 78, American comic book writer and editor for DC Comics.[17]
  • Doug Serrurier, 85, South African former Grand Prix racing driver and constructor.[18]

4[edit]

  • Alec Bregonzi, 76, British actor.[19]
  • Bill Fleming, 92, American MLB pitcher for the Boston Red Sox and Chicago Cubs[20]
  • Ron Jones, 41, American Major League Baseball player, brain hemorrhage.[21]
  • Richard Kapp, 69, American conductor and founder of the Philharmonia Virtuosi.[22]
  • John Kerr, 46, British footballer (Tranmere Rovers).[23]
  • Anthony Marreco, 90, British barrister, junior Counsel at the Nuremberg Trials and founding member of Amnesty International.[24]
  • Sir John Rowlands, 90, British air marshal and George Cross recipient.[25]
  • William M. Steger, 85, United States district court judge and Republican candidate for Governor of Texas in 1960.[26]

5[edit]

  • Frederick Franck, 97, Dutch artist, author, and dentist.[27]
  • Elizabeth Fretwell, 85, Australian opera singer best known for her performances with the Sadler's Wells company.[28]
  • Eric Gregg, 55, American former Major League Baseball umpire, stroke.[29]
  • Edward L. Moyers, 77, American railroad executive.[30]
  • Robert Ross, 86, American leader of the Muscular Dystrophy Association for 44 years and persuaded Jerry Lewis to undertake a yearly telethon to raise money for muscular dystrophy, complications of broken hip.[31]
  • Harley Rutledge, 80, American physicist and ufologist.[32]
  • Huda Sultan, 80, Egyptian actress, cancer.[33]

6[edit]

  • Leslie Alcock, 81, British pioneer of Dark Age archaeology, led the team that excavated Cadbury Castle.[34]
  • María Teresa López Boegeholz, 78, Chilean oceanographer.[35]
  • Arnold Newman, 88, American photographer who pioneered "environmental portraiture".[36]
  • Billy Preston, 59, American musician ("You Are So Beautiful", "Nothing from Nothing") known for his work with the Beatles, malignant hypertension leading to kidney failure.[37]
  • Hilton Ruiz, 54, American jazz pianist, injuries from a fall.[38]
  • Léon Weil, 109, French World War I veteran.[39]
  • Jason Moss, 31, American attorney and author of the book "The Last Victim"[40]

7[edit]

  • Abu Musab al-Zarqawi, 39, Jordanian leader of Al-Qaeda in Iraq, US military strike.[41]
  • Sheik Abd-Al-Rahman, spiritual adviser for Al-Qaeda in Iraq, US military strike.[42]
  • Roy Brain, 79, Australian cricketer.[43]
  • Terry McCann, 74, American wrestler, olympic gold medalist in freestyle wrestling and helped found USA Wrestling, and retired Executive Director of Toastmasters International, cancer.[44]
  • Ingo Preminger, 95, Austrian-born American Hollywood talent agent and producer (M*A*S*H), brother of Otto Preminger.[45][46]
  • Mickey Sims, 51, American football defensive tackle, former player with the Cleveland Browns, heart attack.[47]
  • Louis B. Sohn, 92, Ukrainian-born scholar of international law, helped draft the UN Charter.[48]
  • John Tenta (aka "Earthquake"), 42, Canadian professional wrestler for the World Wrestling Federation, bladder cancer.[49]

8[edit]

  • Jake Copass, 86, American cowboy poet, leukemia.[50]
  • Robert Donner, 75, American character actor probably best known for playing Exidor on Mork and Mindy, aneurysm.[51]

  • Mykola Kolessa, 102, Ukrainian composer and conductor.[53]
  • Abouna Matta El Meskeen, 87, Egyptian Coptic Orthodox monk, Spiritual Father of St. Macarius' Monastery in the Wilderness of Scetis, Egypt.[54]

  • Jamal Abu Samhadana, Palestinian leader of PA / Hamas forces in Gaza Strip and PRC. Killed by Israeli air strike.[56]
  • Talcott Seelye, 84, United States Foreign Service Officer and ambassador to Tunisia and Syria.[57]
  • Sir Peter Smithers, 92, British politician, MP for Winchester and Secretary General of the Council of Europe.[58]

9[edit]

  • Drafi Deutscher, 60, German singer.
  • Michael Forrestall, 73, Canadian senator, died following hospitalization for breathing problems.[60]
  • Patricia Janus, 74, American poet, heart attack brought on by liver cancer.
  • Enzo Siciliano, 72, Italian writer, diabetes mellitus.[61]
  • Vern Williams, 76, American bluegrass mandolin player and singer.[62]

  • Ruddy Thomas, 54, Jamaican singer, heart attack.[72]
