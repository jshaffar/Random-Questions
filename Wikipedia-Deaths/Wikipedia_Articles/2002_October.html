The following is a list of notable deaths in October 2002.

Entries for each day are listed alphabetically by surname. A typical entry lists information in the following sequence:

  • Name, age, country of citizenship at birth, subsequent country of citizenship (if applicable), reason for notability, cause of death (if known), and reference.

October 2002[edit]

1[edit]

  • Walter Annenberg, 94, American publisher (The Philadelphia Inquirer, TV Guide, Daily Racing Form, Seventeen) and philanthropist.[1]
  • Ilie Ceaușescu, Romanian general and communist politician
  • Consuelo Salgar, 74, Colombian journalist, advertising executive, and politician, liver cancer.
  • Ted Serong, 86, Australian soldier.

2[edit]

  • Ted Blackman, 60, Canadian media personality in Montreal, Quebec.[2]
  • Norman O. Brown, 89, American philosopher and author (Life Against Death, Love's Body).[3]
  • Heinz von Foerster, 90, Austrian-American physicist and philosopher, one of the founders of constructivism.[4]
  • Alexander Sinclair, 91, Canadian ice hockey player.

3[edit]

  • Cliff Durandt, 62, South African football player, heart attack.
  • John Erritt, 71, British civil servant.
  • Bruce Paltrow, 58, American television and film director and producer, complications from oral cancer and pneumonia.
  • Dalvanius Prime, 54, New Zealand entertainer and songwriter, cancer.
  • John Weitz, 79, American fashion designer, novelist and historian.[5]

4[edit]

  • Alphonse Chapanis, 85, American pioneer in the field of industrial design.[6]
  • André Delvaux, 76, Belgian film director, considered the father of the Belgian film industry.[7]
  • Fram Farrington, 94, Northern Irish scientific officer, member of a secret World War II Antarctic expeditionary force.[8]
  • Barbara Fawkes, 87, British nurse, Chief Education Officer for the General Nursing Council for England and Wales.[9]
  • Ahmad Mahmoud, 70, Iranian novelist.
  • Roy Wilkins, 68, American professional football player (University of Georgia, Los Angeles Rams, Washington Redskins).[10]

5[edit]

  • Sir Reginald Hibbert, 80, British diplomat.
  • Tony Mazzocchi, 76, American labor leader.
  • Laurence Schache, 34, Australian rules footballer, amyloidosis.
  • Victor Zagainov, 49, hot air balloon pilot and astronomer from Kazakhstan, ballooning accident.

6[edit]

  • Frank Barron, 80, American psychologist and author, considered a pioneer in the psychology of the creative mind.[11]
  • Claus von Amsberg, 76, husband of Queen Beatrix of the Netherlands and Dutch diplomat.[12]
  • Ben Eastman, 91, American runner (silver medal in men's 400 metres at the 1932 Summer Olympics).[13]
  • Chuck Rayner, 82, Canadian professional hockey player (New York Americans, New York Rangers).[14]
  • Nick Whitehead, 69, British (Welsh) sprinter (bronze medal in men's 4 × 100 metres relay at the 1960 Summer Olympics).[15]

7[edit]

  • Pierangelo Bertoli, 59, Italian singer-songwriter and poet, heart attack.
  • Oran K. Gragson, 91, American businessman and longest-serving mayor of Las Vegas.
  • Marcel Paille, 69, Canadian ice hockey goaltender, cancer.
  • Ed Rossbach, 88, American fiber artist

8[edit]

  • Jodie Beeler, 80, American baseball player (Cincinnati Reds).[16]
  • Phyllis Calvert, 87, British actress (The Man in Grey, Fanny by Gaslight, The Magic Bow, My Own True Love).[17]
  • Lucille Caudill Little, 93, American patron of the arts and philanthropist.
  • Jacques Richard, 50, Canadian ice hockey player, car accident.
  • Joachim Zahn, 88, German business executive, a senior executive at Daimler-Benz from 1958 to 1980.[18]

9[edit]

  • Anwar Hussain, 82, Pakistani cricketer.
  • Oleksandr Liashko, 86, Ukrainian politician.
  • Jim Martin, 78, American football player.
  • Aileen Wuornos, 46, convicted of killing six men, lethal injection.

10[edit]

  • Allison Calder, 42, New Zealand Olympic swimmer (women's 400 metre freestyle, women's 800 metre freestyle at the 1976 Summer Olympics).[19]
  • Tom Casey, 78, American professional football player (New York Yankees, Hamilton Wildcats, Winnipeg Blue Bombers).[20]
  • Fate Echols, 63, American professional football player (Northwestern University, St. Louis Cardinals).[21]
  • Lawrence H. Fountain, 89, American politician (U.S. Representative for North Carolina's 2nd congressional district).[22]
  • Teresa Graves, 54, American actress and singer, house fire.
  • Denison Kitchel, 94, American lawyer political advisor.
  • Zara Nelsova, 81, Canadian cellist.
  • Erling Sørensen, 81, Danish football player and manager.
  • Lady Marguerite Tangye, 89, British debutante, model, and actress.
  • Joe Wood, 86, American baseball player (Boston Red Sox).[23]

11[edit]

  • Shaden Abu-Hijleh, 61, Palestinian peace activist.[24]
  • Sir Stewart Crawford, 89, British diplomat.[25]
  • Paul Crump, 72, American death row inmate and author (Burn, Killer, Burn).[26]
  • William J. Field, 93, British politician.
  • Ron Gray, 82, English football player and manager.
  • Betty Molesworth Allen, 89, New Zealand botanist.
  • Dina Pathak, 80, Indian actor and director, heart attack.
  • Stanley Wagner, 94, Canadian ice hockey player (gold medal in ice hockey at the 1932 Winter Olympics).[27]
  • Rusty Wailes, 66, American rower (two Olympic gold medals in rowing: 1956 men's eight, 1960 men's coxless four).[28]

12[edit]

  • Viktor Asmaev, 54, Russian Olympic equestrian (gold medal winner in equestrian team jumping at the 1980 Summer Olympics).[29]
  • Thomas J. Cahill, 92, American chief of police of San Francisco, California from 1958 to 1970.[30]
  • Ray Conniff, 85, American bandleader and arranger (The Ray Conniff Singers), made more than 100 recordings and produced 25 Top-40 albums.[31]
  • Sir Desmond Fitzpatrick, 89, British general.[32]
  • Audrey Mestre, 28, French world record-setting free diver.
  • Nozomi Momoi, 24, Japanese AV idol, murdered.
  • Sidney W. Pink, 86, American movie director and producer.
  • Mick Shoebottom, 57, English rugby league player.

13[edit]

  • Stephen Ambrose, 66, American historian, presidential biographer and author of Band of Brothers.[33]
  • Keene Curtis, 79, American actor (The Rothschilds, Annie, Cheers).[34]
  • Jim Higgins, 71, British politician.

14[edit]

  • Jim Brakefield, 83, American college football and baseball coach (Wofford College, Appalachian State University).[35]
  • Irving Davies, 76, Welsh dancer and choreographer (Value for Money, Kiss Me Kate, The Benny Hill Show).[36]
  • Elisabeth Furse, 92, German-British Communist activist and World War II resistance worker.[37]
  • Oluf Reed-Olsen, 84, Norwegian pilot.
  • S. William Green, 72, American politician (U.S. Representative for New York's 18th and 15th congressional districts).[38]
  • Arturo Silvestri, 81, Italian football player and manager.
  • Harriet Werley, 88, American nurse and nurse researcher, a pioneer in nursing informatics.[39]

15[edit]

  • Grace Hamblin, British private secretary to Winston Churchill and Lady Clementine Churchill.[40]
  • Jack Lee, 89, British film director.
  • Ze'ev, 79, Israeli caricaturist and illustrator, awarded the Israel Prize for Journalism.[41]

16[edit]

  • Per Bak, 53, Danish theoretical physicist, known for "self-organized criticality", myelodysplastic syndrome.[42]
  • Philip Brett, 64, British-born American musicologist, musician and conductor, cancer.[43]
  • James Disbrow, 54, American figure skater, co-founder of Buffalo Wild Wings, and president of the United States Figure Skating Association.[44]
  • William Macmillan, 75, Scottish minister.

17[edit]

  • Bashful Brother Oswald, 90, American country musician, a frequent Grand Ole Opry performer.[45]
  • Derek Bell, 66, Northern Irish harpist, pianist, oboist, musicologist and composer (The Chieftains), cardiac arrest.[46]
  • Pattie Coldwell, 50, British television broadcaster and journalist (Nationwide, Open Air, Loose Women, You and Yours).[47]
  • Yara Cortes, 81, Brazilian actress.
  • Zekerijah Đezić, 64, Bosnian folk singer.
  • Jeanne L. Noble, 76, African-American educator, congestive heart failure.
  • Yitzhak Peretz, 66, Israeli politician.
  • Alina Pienkowska, 50, Polish free trade union activist and politician, cancer.
  • Henri Renaud, 67, French jazz pianist and record company executive.
  • Aileen Riggin, 96, American swimmer and diver.
  • Catherine Uhlmyer, 109, longest-living survivor of the General Slocum fire of June 15, 1904.

18[edit]

  • Richard Bernstein, 62, American artist, member of the circle of Andy Warhol, complications of AIDS.[48]
  • Sir Cecil Blacker, 86, British army general, Adjutant-General to the Forces.[49]
  • Kam Fong Chun, 84, American police officer and actor (Hawaii Five-O).[50]
  • John D. Ferry, 90, Canadian-American biochemist, made important contributions to polymer science.[51]
  • Roman Tam, 52, Hong Kong canto-pop singer.

19[edit]

  • Manuel Álvarez Bravo, 100, Mexican photographer.[52]
  • Peter Bergmann, 87, German-American physicist, known for his work with Albert Einstein on a unified field theory.[53]
  • Maitland B. Bleecker, 99, American inventor and author.
  • Mehli Mehta, 94, Indian conductor and violinist.
  • Hank Smith, 68, Canadian country music singer.

20[edit]

  • Barbara Berjer, 82, American actress (As the World Turns, Another World).[54]
  • Les Douglas, 83, Canadian professional ice hockey player (Detroit Red Wings).[55]
  • Bernard Fresson, 71, French actor (French Connection II, The Tenant, Street of No Return).[56]
  • Mel Harder, 93, American baseball player (Cleveland Indians), coach and manager (Cleveland Indians).[57]
  • Bernhard Neumann, 93, British-Australian mathematician.
  • Nolan White, 71, American land speed record race car driver.[58]

21[edit]

  • Sir Roger Cork, 55, British accountant and Lord Mayor of London.[59]
  • Manfred Ewald, 76, East German Olympic committee president, convicted for his role in the use of PEDs by East German athletes.[60]
  • Anna King, 64, American soul and gospel singer.
  • Marquita Rivera, 80, Puerto Rican actress, singer and dancer, stroke.
  • Beatrice Serota, Baroness Serota, 83, British politician.
  • Eileen Simpson, American writer and psychotherapist.
  • Harbhajan Singh, 82, Punjabi poet, critic, cultural commentator, and translator.
  • Y. R. Swamy, Indian film director and screenwriter.

22[edit]

  • Marian Bergeron, 84, American beauty pageant winner (Miss America 1933) and big band singer (Rudy Vallée, Tommy Dorsey, Guy Lombardo).[61]
  • Geraldine of Albania, 87, Queen consort of King Zog I of Albania.
  • Sir Herbert Duffus, 94, Jamaican jurist.
  • Igor Irodov, 78, Soviet Russian physicist and World War II veteran.
  • Nonoy Marcelo, 63, Filipino cartoonist, complications from diabetes.

23[edit]

  • Lucille Carroll, 96, American Broadway actress and MGM studio executive.[62]
  • Adolph Green, 87, American lyricist and playwright.
  • Richard Helms, 89, American former CIA director.
  • David Lewis, 85, New Zealand sailor and adventurer.
  • Elizabeth Pakenham, Countess of Longford, 96, British historian.

24[edit]

  • Scott Plank, 43, American actor.
  • Winton M. Blount, 81, American public servant (United States Postmaster General), business executive and philanthropist.[63]
  • Harry Hay, 90, American gay rights activist and Mattachine Society founder.
  • Lotte Tarp, 57, Danish actress.

25[edit]

  • Ian Russell, 13th Duke of Bedford, 85, British peer and writer.[64]
  • Richard Harris, 72, Irish actor.
  • René Thom, 79, French mathematician.
  • Ruth B. Weg, 82, American professor of gerontology.[65]
  • Paul Wellstone, 58, American professor, author and politician (U.S. Senator from Minnesota).[66]

26[edit]

  • Movsar Barayev, 23, Chechen Islamist militia leader, killed during the Moscow theater hostage crisis.
  • Zura Barayeva, Chechen Islamist, killed during the Moscow theater hostage crisis.
  • Jacques Massu, 94, French general.
  • George Serdula, 82, American football player, coach, and university professor.
  • Sally Hoyt Spofford, 88, American ornithologist.
  • William A. Zeck, 87, American New York State judge and prosecutor at the Nuremberg war-crimes trials.[67]

27[edit]

  • Andre DeToth, 89, Hungarian-American film director (The Gunfighter, House of Wax, The Indian Fighter), aneurysm.[68]
  • Tom Dowd, 77, American recording engineer and producer, a pioneer in stereo and multitrack tape recording, emphysema.[69]
  • Edor Hjukström, 86, Swedish military officer and Olympic skier.
  • Baby Lloyd Stallworth, 61, American singer, dancer, songwriter, musician, choreographer, and recording artist, complications of diabetes.
  • Charles Orville Whitley, 75, American politician (U.S. Representative for North Carolina's 3rd congressional district).[70]

28[edit]

  • Joan Archibald, 89, Canadian Olympic fencer (women's foil at the 1932 Summer Olympics).[71]
  • Margaret Booth, 104, American Academy Award-winning film editor (Mutiny on the Bounty, The Way We Were, The Sunshine Boys, The Goodbye Girl, Annie).[72]
  • Morris Curotta, 73, Australian Olympic sprinter (1948 Summer Olympics, 1952 Summer Olympics).[73]
  • Lawrence Dobkin, 83, American television director and character actor (The Ten Commandments, The Defiant Ones, North by Northwest, Patton).[74]
  • Laurence Foley, 60, American diplomat and employee of U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID).[75]
  • Erling Persson, 85, Swedish businessman, founder of H&M.
  • Sir Patrick Russell, 76, British jurist.

29[edit]

  • Muriel Bevis, 74, American professional baseball player (All-American Girls Professional Baseball League).[76]
  • Marion Carpenter, 82, American press photographer, one of the first women White House photographers, covered President Harry Truman.[77]
  • Chang-Lin Tien, 67, Chinese-American educator, 7th Chancellor of the University of California, Berkeley.
  • Richard Jenkin, 77, Cornish nationalist politician.
  • Glenn McQueen, 41, Canadian film animator.
  • Julian Webb, 91, American lawyer, politician and judge (Georgia Court of Appeals).[78]

30[edit]

  • Pierre Aigrain, 78, French physicist.[79]
  • Alfred Atherton, 80, American Foreign Service Officer and diplomat, served as United States Ambassador to Egypt from 1979 to 1983.[80]
  • Aliki Diplarakou, 90, Greek beauty pageant winner, first Greek contestant to win Miss Europe title.[81]
  • Jam Master Jay, 37, American musician and DJ of Run DMC, murdered.[82]
  • Sir William Mitchell, 77, British physicist.

31[edit]

  • Yuri Ahronovitch, 70, Russian conductor.
  • Moose Cholak, 72, American professional wrestler.[83]
  • Sir Napier Crookenden, 87, British Army general.[84]
  • Jean-Marie Fortier, 82, Canadian Roman Catholic prelate.[85]
  • Baroness Hylton-Foster, 94, British peer.
  • Raymond Savignac, 94, French graphic designer.
  • Eckart Wagner, 64, German Olympic sailor (1960 Star sailing, 1964 5.5 metre sailing, 1968 Star sailing).[86]
