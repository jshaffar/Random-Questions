The following is a list of notable deaths in August 2001.

Entries for each day are listed alphabetically by surname. A typical entry lists information in the following sequence:

  • Name, age, country of citizenship at birth, subsequent country of citizenship (if applicable), reason for notability, cause of death (if known), and reference.

August 2001[edit]

1[edit]

  • Jay Chamberlain, 77, American racing driver.
  • Dwight Eddleman, 78, American basketball player and Olympic athlete.
  • Joe Lynch, 76, Irish actor.
  • Korey Stringer, 27, American football player (Ohio State, Minnesota Vikings), complications brought on by heat stroke.[1]

2[edit]

  • James A. Corbett, 67, American rancher and philosopher.
  • Valerie Davies, 89, British Olympic swimmer, bronze medalist (1932).
  • Sir Edward Gardner, 89, British politician.[2]
  • Ronald Townson, 68, American vocalist (The 5th Dimension).[3]

3[edit]

  • Henriette Bie Lorentzen, 90, Norwegian activist.
  • Louis Chevalier, 90, French historian with interests in geography, demography and sociology.[4]
  • Christopher Hewett, 80, British actor (Mr. Belvedere).[5]
  • Lars Johan Werle, 75, Swedish composer.

4[edit]

  • S. K. Bhatnagar, 71, Indian politician and diplomat.
  • Joseph Cooper, 88, British pianist and broadcaster.
  • Lorenzo Music, 64, American voice actor known for the voice of the cartoon cat Garfield, complications related to lung and bone cancer.[6]

5[edit]

  • Otema Allimadi, 72, Ugandan Foreign Minister (1979–1980) and Prime Minister of Uganda (1980–1985).[7]
  • Iskra Babich, 69, Soviet film director and screenwriter.
  • Caro Crawford Brown, 93, American journalist and Pulitzer Prize winner.[8]
  • Roy D. Chapin Jr., 85, American business executive (Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of American Motors Corporation).[9]
  • Bahne Rabe, 37, German rower, Olympic champion (1988).[10]

6[edit]

  • Jorge Amado, 88, Brazilian writer.[11]
  • Hans Gruber, 76, Austrian-Canadian conductor (Victoria Symphony, University of Toronto).[12]
  • Vasili Kuznetsov, 69. Russian decathlete.
  • Kenneth MacDonald, 50, English actor.
  • Jim Mallory, 82, American baseball player and football coach.[13]
  • Wilhelm Mohnke, 90, German general during the Nazi era.
  • Alan Rafkin, 73, American actor, director and producer.
  • Dame Dorothy Tutin, 71, British actress (The Importance of Being Earnest, The Beggar's Opera, A Tale of Two Cities, The Shooting Party).[14]

7[edit]

  • Dan Edwards, 75, American professional football player (1948–1957) and coach (1958–1961).[15]
  • Jack James, 80, American rocket engineer who worked at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (Project Manager for NASA's Mariner program).[16]
  • Robert Kraus, 76, American children's author and cartoonist.

8[edit]

  • John Deacon, 38, British motorcycle racer, motorcycle accident.
  • Jean Dorst, 77, French ornithologist, former director of the National Museum of Natural History in Paris.[17]
  • John A. Hostetler, 82, American scholar.
  • George Mann, 83, English cricketer.
  • Peter Sinclair, 62, New Zealand radio personality.
  • Patrick David Wall, 76, British neuroscientist.

9[edit]

  • Abe Bonnema, 74, Dutch architect.
  • Humphry Bowen, 72, British botanist and chemist.
  • Jacky Boxberger, French athlete, killed by an elephant[18]
  • Elmer Knutson, 86, Canadian businessman, activist and politician.
  • John Gordon Lane, 85, Canadian politician.
  • Sir Alec Skempton, 87, British scientist.

10[edit]

  • Lou Boudreau, 84, American baseball player and manager, seven-time All-Star, 1948 American League Most Valuable Player and a member of the Baseball Hall of Fame.[19]
  • Manfred Eglin, 65, German footballer.
  • Edward Gaskin, 83, Panamanian educator and labor leader.
  • Bob Johnson, 60, British businessman and philanthropist.[20]
  • Ramón Monzant, 68, Venezuelan baseball player.[21]

11[edit]

  • Paul Cunniffe, 40, Irish singer-songwriter, fall from balcony.
  • Edward Thomas Hall, 77, British scientist, known for exposing the Piltdown Man as a fraud.[22]
  • James Lechay, 94, American painter.
  • Barbara Ruszczyc, 72, Polish Egyptologist and art historian.
  • Percy Stallard, 92, British racing cyclist.[23]

12[edit]

  • Irene Astor, Baroness Astor of Hever, 81, English noblewoman and philanthropist.
  • Julian Pitt-Rivers, 82, British social anthropologist ands ethnographer.[24]
  • Sir Walter Walker, 88, British army general.

13[edit]

  • John C. Elliott, 82, American politician and 39th Governor of American Samoa.
  • Sir John Hoddinott, British police officer.
  • Jim Hughes, 78, American baseball player.[25]
  • R.S. Jones, 47, American novelist and editor (HarperCollins Publishers).[26]
  • Otto Stuppacher, 54, Austrian race car driver.[27]

14[edit]

  • Earl Anthony, 63, American professional bowler.[28]
  • Missy Cleveland, 41, Playboy Playmate.
  • Oscar Janiger, 83, American experimental psychiatrist, known for his LSD research.[29]
  • Sir Graham Shillington, 90, Northern Irish police officer.[30]

15[edit]

  • Richard Chelimo, 29, Kenyan Olympic long-distance runner (silver medal winner of the men's 10,000 metres at the 1992 Summer Olympics).[31]
  • Raymond Edward Johnson, 90, American radio and stage actor (Inner Sanctum Mysteries).[32]
  • Jim Russell, 92, Australian cartoonist.
  • Sir Roderick Sarell, 88, British diplomat.

16[edit]

  • Dave Barry, 82, American actor and comedian.
  • Kenneth Reese Cole Jr., 63, American political aide to Richard Nixon.
  • Fred Glover, 73, Canadian professional ice hockey player (Chicago Black Hawks, Detroit Red Wings, Cleveland Barons) and coach (Oakland Seals, Los Angeles Kings).[33]
  • Kaadsiddheshwar, 96, Indian Hindu guru.
  • Anna Mani, 82, Indian physicist and meteorologist.
  • Floyd Spence, 73, American attorney and a politician, cerebral thrombosis.
  • Sidney Tillim, 76, American artist and art critic.

17[edit]

  • Josef Fried, 87, Polish-American organic chemist, member of the National Academy of Sciences and the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.[34]
  • Herman Goffberg, 80, American Olympic long-distance runner (men's 10,000 metres at the 1948 Summer Olympics).[35]
  • Emil Gorovets, 78, Soviet Ukrainian singer.
  • Živko Nikolić, 59, Yugoslav and Montenegrin film director.
  • Flip Phillips, 86, American jazz tenor saxophone and clarinet player.[36]
  • Sir Ralph Verney, 5th Baronet, 86, British army officer and conservationist.

18[edit]

  • Jack Elliott, 74, American film and television music composer, conductor and arranger (Barney Miller, Charlie's Angels, The Love Boat, The Jerk, Oh God!).[37]
  • David Peakall, 70, British environmental toxicologist and ornithologist.[38]
  • Toppur Seethapathy Sadasivan, 88, Indian plant pathologist.

19[edit]

  • Betty Everett, 61, American soul singer and pianist ("The Shoop Shoop Song", "Let It Be Me").[39]
  • Les Sealey, 43, English footballer, heart attack.
  • Inder Singh, 57, Indian Olympic hockey player.
  • Willy Vannitsen, 66, Belgian racing cyclist.
  • Donald Woods, 67, South African journalist, newspaper editor, and anti-apartheid activist, made famous by exposing the killing of his friend, Steve Biko.[40]

20[edit]

  • Richard Cloward, 74, American sociologist and activist (National Voter Registration Act of 1993).[41]
  • Hazzard Dill, 82, Bermudian Olympic sprinter (1948 Summer Olympics).[42]
  • Sir Fred Hoyle, 86, British astronomer and science fiction writer.[43]
  • Walter Reed, 85, American stage, film and television actor.[44]
  • Kim Stanley, 76, American actress (two nominations for a Tony Award, two nominations for an Academy Award, winner of a Primetime Emmy Award).[45]

21[edit]

  • Beryl Cooke, 94, British actress.
  • Pál Engel, 63, Hungarian historian.
  • Steven Izenour, 61, American architect and author (Learning from Las Vegas).[46]
  • Calum MacKay, 74, Canadian professional ice hockey player.[47]
  • Norman Rigby, 78, English footballer.

22[edit]

  • Tatyana Averina, 51, Soviet Russian Olympic speed skater (won two gold medals and two bronze medals at the 1976 Winter Olympics).[48]
  • Rose Edgcumbe, 67, British psychologist, psychoanalyst, and academic.[49]
  • Bernard Heuvelmans, 84, French scientist.
  • Bobby Johnstone, 71, Scottish footballer (Hibernian, Manchester City, Oldham Athletic, Scotland).
  • Tage Jönsson, 81, Swedish Olympic racewalker (men's 50 kilometres walk at the 1948 Summer Olympics).[50]
  • Gita Luka, 79, Israeli actress, singer and comedian, stroke.[51]

23[edit]

  • Eric Allandale, 65, British jazz musician.
  • Howard Fletcher, 88, American college football player and head coach (Northern Illinois University).[52]
  • Frank Emilio Flynn, 80, Cuban pianist.[53]
  • Ray Frederick, 72, Canadian professional ice hockey player (Chicago Black Hawks).[54]
  • Kathleen Freeman, 82, American actress (Wagon Train, North to Alaska, The Nutty Professor, Support Your Local Sheriff!, The Blues Brothers, The Golden Girls).[55]
  • Herbert Haag, 86, German-Swiss Roman Catholic theologian and biblical scholar (known for challenging the Vatican).[56]
  • Shirley Kleinhans, 72, American baseball player (All-American Girls Professional Baseball League).[57]
  • Peter Maas, 72, American journalist and author (Serpico, The Valachi Papers).[58]
  • Gordon Ogden, 92, Australian rules footballer.

24[edit]

  • Jane Greer, 76, American film and television actress (Out of the Past).[59]
  • Milan Kadlec, 42, Czechoslovakian Olympic pentathlete (team and individual modern pentathlon at the 1980 Summer Olympics and 1988 Summer Olympics).[60]
  • Helen McGrath, 59, Scottish trade unionist.
  • Hank Sauer, 84, American baseball player (1952 Most Valuable Player) ("The Mayor of Wrigley Field").[61]
  • Raymond Wilding-White, 78, American composer.[62]

25[edit]

  • Aaliyah, 22, American R&B singer and actress (Romeo Must Die), plane crash.[63]
  • Raymond Abescat, 109, French veteran of World War I.
  • Mary Barnard, 91, American poet, biographer and translator.
  • Carl Brewer, 62, Canadian ice hockey player.[64]
  • John Chambers, 78, American make-up artist and first civilian to receive the Intelligence Medal of Merit.
  • Diana Golden, 38, American disabled ski racer, cancer.
  • Philippe Léotard, 60, French actor and singer, respiratory failure.[65]
  • Ginzō Matsuo, 50, Japanese voice actor, subarachnoid hemorrhage.
  • John L. Nelson, 85, American jazz musician, songwriter and father of Prince.
  • Asit Sen, 78, Bengali Indian film director, cinematographer and screenwriter.
  • Ken Tyrrell, 75, British motor racing driver and team leader, pancreatic cancer.[66]

26[edit]

  • Louis Muhlstock, 97, Canadian painter.[67]
  • Cecil Null, 74, American songwriter, cancer.
  • Marita Petersen, 60, Prime Minister of the Faroe Islands and first female speaker of the House, cancer.
  • Al Pittman, 61, Canadian poet and playwright.

27[edit]

  • Michael Dertouzos, 64, Greek-American professor, computer scientist and Director of the MIT Laboratory for Computer Science (LCS) from 1974 to 2001.[68]
  • Juan Lechín Oquendo, 87, Bolivian politician, Vice President (1960–1964).
  • Abu Ali Mustafa, 63, Palestinian leader and Secretary General of the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine (PFLP).[69]

28[edit]

  • James Homer Elledge, 58, American convicted murderer, executed by lethal injection in Washington.
  • Bert Gardiner, 88, Canadian professional ice hockey player (Montreal Canadiens, Chicago Black Hawks, Boston Bruins, New York Rangers).[70]
  • Käthe Grasegger, 84, German Olympic alpine skier (silver medal winner in women's combined alpine skiing at the 1936 Winter Olympics).[71]
  • Juan Muñoz, 48, Spanish sculptor.
  • Serhiy Perkhun, 23, Ukrainian footballer.
  • Sir Reo Stakis, 88, Cypriot-born British hotelier.

29[edit]

  • Roger Daley, 58, British meteorologist.[72]
  • Victor Jörgensen, 77, Danish Olympic boxer (bronze medal winner in welterweight boxing at the 1952 Summer Olympics).[73]
  • Sid Peterson, 83, American baseball player.[74]
  • Francisco Rabal, 75, Spanish actor.[75]
  • Dick Selma, 57, American baseball player.[76]
  • Graeme "Shirley" Strachan, 50, Australian singer (Skyhooks) and television presenter.[77]
  • Eric Tipton, 86, American baseball player.[78]
  • Dame Olga Uvarov, 91, Russian-born British veterinarian.

30[edit]

  • A. F. M. Ahsanuddin Chowdhury, 86, 9th President of Bangladesh.
  • G. K. Moopanar,70, Indian Politician, Rajya sabha Member[79]
  • Julie Bishop, 87, American actress (Sands of Iwo Jima, Princess O'Rourke, Northern Pursuit, The High and the Mighty).[80]
  • Stan Harland, 61, English football player.
  • Govan Mbeki, 91, South African politician, leader of the ANC and SACP.

31[edit]

  • Sir Eric Bullus, 94, British politician.
  • Crash Davis, 82, American baseball player.[81]
  • Rex Forrester, 72, New Zealand hunting and fishing specialist and outdoor sports author.[82]
  • Paul Hamlyn, Baron Hamlyn, 75, British publisher and philanthropist.
  • James Petrie, 59, British pharmacologist.
